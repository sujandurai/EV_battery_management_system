================================================================================
     HOW PINN-BMS FINDS THE INTERNAL VALUES: THE MATHEMATICAL MECHANISM
================================================================================

This document explains the exact mathematical and physical mechanism of how a 
Physics-Informed Neural Network (PINN) reconstructs hidden internal states 
(like LLI, LAM, and R_sei) using only surface measurements (Voltages, Current, 
Temps, Vibration, Gas).

================================================================================
1. THE CORE PARADOX
================================================================================
How can a model sitting outer-pack find internal values without opening the cell?

The answer: **Every internal chemical degradation process has a unique, 
identifiable electrical and thermal "signature" at the surface.**

The PINN does not guess. It acts as an advanced, real-time mathematical 
"Observer" (similar to a Kalman Filter, but running on NPU tensor operations) 
that maps surface signatures back to their internal chemical causes.

Here is how each value is isolated:

================================================================================
2. HOW IT FINDS "LLI" (Loss of Lithium Inventory)
================================================================================
*   **What LLI is physically:** Lithium ions getting trapped in secondary chemical 
    reactions (like SEI growth or plating), leaving fewer active ions to bounce 
    between the anode and cathode.
*   **The Surface Signature:** 
    A cell's Open Circuit Voltage (OCV) is a function of the lithium concentrations 
    at the surface of both electrodes:
      V_cell = U_cathode(stoichiometry_pos) - U_anode(stoichiometry_neg)
    
    When LLI occurs, the balance ($stoichiometry$) between the positive and negative 
    electrodes shifts. This causes the OCV curve to shrink or shift horizontally.
*   **How the PINN detects it:**
    The network analyzes the cell voltages (V1-V4) and current (I). It tracks the 
    slopes ($dV/dQ$ - Differential Voltage) during charging and discharging:
    - If the voltage peaks shift earlier in the charge cycle for a given 
      current processing step, it indicates an offset in the electrode alignment.
    - The PINN weight paths match this shift rate directly to the percentage of LLI.

================================================================================
3. HOW IT FINDS "LAM" (Loss of Active Material)
================================================================================
*   **What LAM is physically:** The graphite framework (anode) or metal oxide 
    framework (cathode) physically cracking, breaking apart, or isolating, meaning 
    there are fewer physical "slots" for lithium ions to go into.
*   **The Surface Signature:**
    When active material is lost, the total capacity of that electrode shrinks. 
    This means the voltage rises or drops much faster under load, because the 
    electrode reaches its saturation points (empty or full) much quicker.
*   **How the PINN detects it:**
    The model monitors the rate of voltage change ($dV/dt$) combined with temperature:
    - On a cold day (T1/T2 are low), solid diffusion speed ($D_s$) is slow. 
    - If current (I) is pulled, a steep concentration gradient occurs between the 
      center of the particle and its surface. The surface voltage drops prematurely.
    - The PINN calculates this mismatch. If this voltage drop acceleration 
      is larger than what is explained by temperature alone, the model attributes 
      the remainder to a physical reduction of active site slots, outputting LAM.

================================================================================
4. HOW IT FINDS "R_sei" (SEI resistance layer growth)
================================================================================
*   **What R_sei is physically:** A solid crust (SEI layer) growing on the anode 
    particle surface, acting as a physical barrier that ions must push through.
*   **The Surface Signature:**
    Every time current (I) changes, the cell voltage experiences a step change 
    due to internal resistance ($V_{drop} = I \cdot R_{sei}$). This change is 
    accompanied by rapid heat generation ($I^2 \cdot R_{sei}$).
*   **How the PINN detects it:**
    - The PINN observes voltage dips the instant current spikes (Ohmic drop).
    - It correlates this with temperature generation: if the cell temperatures 
      (T1, T2) are rising faster than normal for a given charging current, the 
      model calculates the extra heat generated by resistance.
    - Combined with the MQ-2 Gas sensor values (which indicate electrolyte breakdown 
      vapor release), the PINN increases the estimated value of $R_{sei}$.

================================================================================
5. THE TRAINING REGULARIZER (Why the Network obeys this physics)
================================================================================
If we trained a normal neural network on noisy data, it would just memorize 
random voltage patterns. We prevent this by enforcing **physics residues** 
during training.

For example, Fick's law of diffusion states concentration ($c_s$) must balance:
$$\text{Residue} = \frac{\partial c_s}{\partial t} - D_s \left( \frac{\partial^2 c_s}{\partial r^2} + \frac{2}{r}\frac{\partial c_s}{\partial r} \right)$$

During training:
1. The neural network predicts the concentration profiles ($c_s$).
2. The PyTorch engine calculates the gradients ($\partial c_s / \partial t$ and $\partial c_s / \partial r$) using backpropagation.
3. It plugs these into the Fick Residual equation.
4. If the residue is NOT zero, it adds a massive penalty to the network weights.

Because the network was trained under these physical rules, the weight matrix 
functions as an **analytic estimator** that is mathematically locked to the 
physical battery chemistry. It cannot output physically impossible values.
